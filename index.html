The Loch Ness Monster

The Loch Ness Monster is a creature of Scottish Folklore said to inhabit the
the Loch Ness in the highlands of Scotland.

The earliest known account of a monster potentially inhabiting the Loch was in the 4th century.

Recounted in the biography of St. Columbia written by Adomnan, the Irish monk St. Columbia witnessed a group of local residents burying a man beside the River Ness. They explained he was mauled and dragged underwater by a "water beast," their rescue attempts futile against it.

1972-2007 | Robert Rines

Dr Robert Rines held many notable titles - lawyer, inventor, musician, composer... but he was most famous for his pursuit of the legendary creature of the loch.

His quest first started in 1972. While on honeymoon with his wife, they were having tea with a friend whose home overlooked the loch when they witnessed a big, grayish hump with elephant-skin-esque texture rising out of the water on four feet and appeared to be about 30 feet long. They stared at the creature for 10 minutes before it disappeared back into the depths of the loch.

After that, Rines made many return trips to the loch in the hopes of locating the elusive "Nessie," even utilising advanced sonar techniques to aid his pursuits. In 1975, Rines successfully photographed what he believed to be the flipper of the creature (pictured above) using a camera connected to a strobe-light system.

Rines continued investigations up until 2007. Though all his investigations ultimately proved inconclusive, his witness accounts and efforts into the uncovering of the truth of the Loch Ness Monster live on in notability.

2011 | Marcus Atkinson

In 2011, boat skipper Marcus Atkinson took a grainy shot of what appears to be a 5ft-wide serpent-like creature from a recording 75ft deep into the loch at Urquhart Bay.

“The object got bigger and bigger, and I took a picture of it with my mobile phone. I was in shock as it looked like a big serpent."

“You can’t fake a sonar image and I’ve never seen anything like this on the fish finder.
I’ve shown it to other experienced skippers and none of us know what it is.”

BIGFOOT

Also known as "Sasquatch," Bigfoot is a large ape-like creature
whose home is supposedly in the forests of North America.

The legacy of a supposed ape-like creature inhabiting the forest of North America stretches back long before there was a concrete name for the creature. Petroglyphs created by a group of Indigenous Americans known as the "Yokuts" depict a group of alleged Bigfoots called "The Family." The largest of these glyphs is called "Hairy Man" by the local tribespeople, with its age being estimated at 500 to 1000 years old.

THE PATTERSON GIMLIN FOOTAGE

The Patterson-Gimlin footage is perhaps the most famous "evidence" of the
creature's existence, allegedly depicting a female Bigfoot.

The footage was taken in the early afternoon of Friday October 1967.
Roger Patterson and Robert Gimlin both acutely invested in the legend of "Bigfoot",
were riding upstream along the east bank of Bluff Creek, northwest of Orleans
California when they spoted the "Bigfoot" figure crouched beside the creek to their left.

CHUPACABRA

The Chupacabra, literally translating to "goat-sucker" from Spanish, is a legendary creature originating
from the folklore of the Americas. It was dubbed the "goat-sucker" from its alleged vampirism;
reported to have attacked and sucked the blood of livestock, including goats and sheep.

The first reported incident possibly relating to a Chupacabra was in 1975 where a series of livestock killings took place in the small town of Moca. There was suspicion that the killings were conducted by a Satanic cult and later on, more killings around the island were reported by farms. Through a series of small incisions, the animals' bodies had been bled dry.

It was in March of 1995 that the first report directly attributed to a Chupacabra came up.
In Puerto Rico, eight sheep were discovered dead and completely drained of blood with three puncture wounds in the chest area.

In August of the same year, Madelyne Toletino came forward with eyewitness accounts of the
creature in the Puerto Rican town of Canóvanas where as many as 150 pets and animals had reportedly been killed.
